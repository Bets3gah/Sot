Frequently Asked Questions

Common dermatological conditions and skin health information

What is Eczema and how is it treated?

+

Eczema, also known as atopic dermatitis, is a chronic inflammatory skin condition that causes red, itchy, and inflamed skin. It commonly affects children but can occur at any age.

Treatment options include:

  • Moisturizers to keep skin hydrated
  • Topical corticosteroids for inflammation
  • Avoiding known triggers (allergens, irritants)
  • Antihistamines for itching relief
  • In severe cases, systemic medications may be prescribed

How can I prevent and treat Acne?

+

Acne occurs when hair follicles become clogged with oil and dead skin cells, leading to whiteheads, blackheads, and inflamed pimples.

Prevention and treatment:

  • Gentle cleansing twice daily with mild cleanser
  • Use non-comedogenic skincare products
  • Topical treatments: benzoyl peroxide, salicylic acid, retinoids
  • Avoid picking or squeezing pimples
  • For severe acne, oral medications may be necessary
  • Maintain a healthy diet and manage stress

What are the warning signs of Skin Cancer?

+

Early detection of skin cancer is crucial for successful treatment. Follow the ABCDE rule for melanoma detection:

  • Asymmetry - One half doesn't match the other
  • Border irregularity - Edges are ragged or blurred
  • Color variation - Multiple colors or unusual colors
  • Diameter - Larger than 6mm (pencil eraser size)
  • Evolving - Changes in size, shape, color, or symptoms

See a dermatologist immediately if you notice any suspicious changes in moles or new growths.

How do I manage Psoriasis symptoms?

+

Psoriasis is an autoimmune condition causing rapid skin cell turnover, resulting in thick, scaly patches.

Management strategies:

  • Topical treatments: corticosteroids, vitamin D analogues
  • Moisturizing regularly to prevent dryness and cracking
  • Light therapy (phototherapy) under medical supervision
  • Systemic medications for moderate to severe cases
  • Stress management and lifestyle modifications
  • Avoiding triggers like infections, stress, and certain medications

What causes Rosacea and how is it managed?

+

Rosacea is a chronic inflammatory skin condition affecting the central face, causing redness, visible blood vessels, and sometimes acne-like bumps.

Management approaches:

  • Identify and avoid personal triggers (spicy foods, alcohol, sun exposure)
  • Use gentle, fragrance-free skincare products
  • Apply broad-spectrum sunscreen daily
  • Topical antibiotics or anti-inflammatory medications
  • Oral antibiotics for moderate to severe cases
  • Laser therapy for visible blood vessels

How important is daily sun protection?

+

Sun protection is essential for preventing skin cancer, premature aging, and other sun-related skin damage.

Effective sun protection includes:

  • Apply broad-spectrum SPF 30+ sunscreen daily
  • Reapply every 2 hours or after swimming/sweating
  • Seek shade during peak UV hours (10 AM - 4 PM)
  • Wear protective clothing, wide-brimmed hats, and UV-blocking sunglasses
  • Avoid tanning beds completely
  • Perform regular self-examinations of your skin
